2019

Crypto-asset: Model price dynamics, portfolio optimization, and option evaluation

Abstract: We demonstrate the construction of an optimal portfolio of crypto assets that minimizes either return variance or conditional value at risk. The portfolio,which outperforms a major stock market index (the S&P 500), can be viewed as a minimum-risk index for such assets. They develop a fair valuation model for options based on a dynamic pricing model for the underlying crypto index. The dynamic model was carefully backtested. The valuation was obtained by passing from the natural world to the equivalent martingale measure via the Esscher transform. While the absence of traded crypto options on the optimal index hinders the comparison of prices obtained from our valuation model to market prices, the authors claim that, if such options on a crypto asset index are introduced, they should follow closely our theoretical prices after adjusting for market frictions and design feature nuances.

2020

Early-warning System on U.S. ETFs

Abstract: Timely and accurate information is ssential in managing investment risk. An early warning system is one that continually tests market risk and has the potential to forecast distressed market periods. There are open questions regarding the capability of early warning systems including signal-to-noise ratio and how early detection can occur. We consider the warning systems based on Mahalanobis distance. We apply this warning systems to historical data for the one general market index (SPY: SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust) and three REIT indices (WILLREITIND, WILLREITPR and FNRE) and compare the results to known global market upheavals.

Performance Attribution on DJI 30

Abstract: This work investigates performance attribution measures as a basis for constraining portfolio optimization. We employ optimizations that minimize conditional value-at-risk and investigate two performance attributes, asset allocation (AA) and the selection effect (SE), as constraints on asset weights. The test portfolio consists of stocks from the Dow Jones Industrial Average index. Values for the performance attributes are established relative to two benchmarks, equi-weighted and price-weighted portfolios of the same stocks. Performance of the optimized portfolios is judged using comparisons of cumulative price and the risk-measures: maximum drawdown, Sharpe ratio, Sortinoâ€“Satchell ratio and Rachev ratio. The results suggest that achieving SE performance thresholds requires larger turnover values than that required for achieving comparable AA thresholds. The results also suggest a positive role in price and risk-measure performance for the imposition of constraints on AA and SE.
